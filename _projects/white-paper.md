## Overview

As part of the ASCTE 201/101 curriculum, I wrote a research white paper arguing that modern footwear design has measurable negative effects on foot health and human biomechanics. The paper required independent sourcing of peer-reviewed research, structured argumentation, and a formal written format. Given my interest in kinesiology and sports medicine, this was one of the most personally relevant assignments I completed at ASCTE.

---

## Abstract

The modern shoe is no longer healthy. Problems include excessive cushioning that numbs sensory feedback, elevated heels that promote poor running form and misdirect force, and narrow toe boxes that impair balance and stability. While solutions exist — primarily barefoot movement and minimalist footwear — they remain poorly understood by the general public. The paper argues that shoe companies have prioritized fashion over biomechanical function, and that this shift is measurably harming the people who wear their products.

---

## Key Arguments

**Cushioning causes injury, not protection**
Modern shoe cushioning numbs the foot's sensory feedback, preventing the wearer from noticing when they are injuring themselves. The excessive padding also forces a heel-strike gait, placing the full impact of each step onto the knees rather than distributing it naturally. Research shows there were almost no running-related injuries before cushioned soles became standard in the 1970s — before then, all shoes had flat bottoms and wide toe boxes.

**Arch support weakens the arch**
The arch of the foot is the body's natural shock absorber. Passive arch support in modern shoes causes the arch's intrinsic muscles to weaken over time, eventually becoming flat and unable to function independently. Arch support was only added to shoes in the 1970s — it is a relatively recent intervention with significant long-term consequences.

**Narrow toe boxes undermine stability**
Human feet are designed so that stability comes from the toes — but only when the toes can splay outward and make full contact with the ground. Modern shoes compress the toes, preventing this, and effectively disabling one of the foot's primary stability mechanisms.

**Elevated heels alter posture and shorten muscle fibers**
Even small heel elevation, found in most everyday shoes, shortens calf muscle fibers over time and shifts body weight forward in ways that stress the lower back and knees. This effect is well documented in weightlifting shoes and high heels, but applies to most modern footwear at a lower level.

**The solution: minimalist footwear and barefoot movement**
Multiple peer-reviewed studies confirm that minimalist shoes allow for a more natural stride length, gait, and foot placement. Barefoot movement is the most effective way to rebuild foot strength and resilience, though minimalist footwear provides a practical middle ground for everyday use.

## What I Learned

Writing a white paper is a different kind of challenge than writing an essay. It requires not just making an argument but finding credible sources that support it, presenting counterarguments fairly, and formatting the work in a way that would hold up to scrutiny from a professional audience. The topic itself was one I cared about — the biomechanics of the human foot and how modern design interferes with natural movement is directly relevant to the kinesiology and physical therapy path I want to pursue.
