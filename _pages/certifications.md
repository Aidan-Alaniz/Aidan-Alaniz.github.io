## Certifications

These are certifications I have earned outside of regular coursework. Each one required dedicated training and a formal assessment to obtain.

---

**Wilderness First Responder (WFR)** · *NOLS*

The WFR is one of the most comprehensive wilderness medicine certifications available to non-physicians. The course covers patient assessment, trauma management, environmental emergencies, and medical conditions in remote settings where definitive care may be hours or days away. Training involved both classroom instruction and hands-on simulated scenarios in the field. Holding a WFR means being trusted to make independent medical decisions and manage patients until evacuation is possible.

---

**Basic Life Support (BLS)** · *American Heart Association*

The BLS certification covers high-quality CPR for adults, children, and infants, use of an AED, and relief of foreign-body airway obstruction. It is the standard certification required for healthcare providers and first responders. Training focused on team-based response and maintaining effective compressions under pressure.

---

**First Aid & CPR** · *American Heart Association*

A foundational emergency response certification covering recognition and response to sudden cardiac arrest, stroke, choking, and other common medical emergencies. Paired with the BLS certification, this ensures a complete baseline of emergency care skills across a wide range of situations.

---

**IS-100.c: Introduction to the Incident Command System** · *FEMA*

This course introduces the Incident Command System (ICS), the standardized management framework used by emergency responders across the US to coordinate response to incidents of any size. The training covers ICS organizational structure, roles and responsibilities, and how resources are managed during an incident. It is a foundational requirement for anyone working in or with emergency management at any level.

---

**IS-200.c: Basic Incident Command System for Initial Response** · *FEMA*

Building on IS-100, this course focuses on applying ICS principles in the field during an initial response. It covers establishing command, managing resources, and coordinating with other agencies in the early stages of an incident. Completing IS-200 qualifies an individual to serve in ICS command and general staff roles during small to medium-scale incidents.
